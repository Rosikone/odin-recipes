Pizza Margherita

Ingredients

  • Flour 200g
  • Water 100ml
  • Brewer's yeast 4g
  • Salt 10g
  • Tomato souce 300g
  • Mozzarella cheese 200g
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Directions

  1. Put the flour on a bowl
  2. Crumble the yeast inside
  3. Pour the water little by little, The right temperature of the water is 25 degrees
  4. When you have added almost all the water, add the salt
  5. Continue to add the water, starting to knead with your hands, to mix the ingredients
  6. Transfer the dough to the work surface and work it with your hands until it is smooth and homogeneous
  7. At this point, let it rest on the work surface for about ten minutes, covering it with the bowl After the leavening time, the dough will be nice and swollen
  8. Once rested, give it a small fold: imagine that the sphere is divided into 4 parts, take the end of each one, pull it gently and fold it towards the center
  9. Once the 4 folds have been made, turn it over on itself
  10. give it the shape of a sphere
  11. Transfer the dough thus formed into a bowl, cover with transparent film
  12. And let rise. For convenience you can put the bowl in the oven off but with the light on, so the inside will reach a temperature of 26-28°, ideal for leavening; alternatively you can also keep the basin in a warm place. Times are approximate since each dough, temperature and weather conditions affect leavening: on average, the dough should take 6 hours to double in volume.
  13. hen transfer it to the work surface and divide it with a tarot into 3 pieces of 265 g
  14. If you need it, you can lightly flour your work surface. Now take each portion and as before, lift a flap of dough and bring it inwards. Repeat the same operation for the other 3 flaps of dough
  15. Turn the dough upside down and proceed with rolling it: rolling the dough means turning it with your hands on the work surface, bringing it towards you and then repeatedly moving it away until you get a smooth and regular ball
  16. Gradually transfer the loaves into a previously greased pizza box and cover whit its lead
  17. Let them rest for another 30 minutes
  18. In the meantime, place the refractory stone in the oven, place it on the top. Turn on the oven bringing the temperature to 250° and activating the static mode. Now using a tarot gently lift the first loaf
  19. Transfer it to a surface with plenty of semolina and add a little more semolina to the surface. Press the dough in the center with your fingertips
  20. Continue in this way making a rotating movement in order to widen the dough
  21. Transfer the dough to the paddle, taking care not to damage it
  22. Now, using a spoon, spread the tomato souce on the pizza, leaving a free edge
  23. Place in the oven, sliding the pizza gently onto the refractory stone and cook for about 6 minutes. Meanwhile, cut the mozzarella into sticks
  24. After 6 minutes, always using the shovel, take the pizza out of the oven
  25. Arrange the mozzarella sticks on top
  26. Put it in the oven for another 6 minutes. When it is golden brown, take it out of the oven, add the basil leaves

    27. Et voila now you have a delicious pizza to enjoi
